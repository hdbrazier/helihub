Choosing the Right Flight School

  • Visit several schools and ask to observe a lesson, speak with current students and take an introductory lesson.
  • Compare 141 vs 61 programs — both lead to the same certificate but differ in structure.
  • Ask about aircraft availability and instructor turnover.

How to Pay for Training

  • Consider flight school financing programs, scholarships, or VA benefits if eligible.
  • Pay-as-you-go may be safer than large upfront payments.
  • Budget realistically — include supplies, written tests, and checkride fees.
  • Take into account living expenses

Example Training Cost & Timeline

This example shows a general timeline and cost breakdown for helicopter flight training from Private through CFII.

Plan for 12–24 months to reach completion of training, depending on training pace, part-time or full-time student.

Prices are approximate. Multiple factors affect the cost, such as location and type of aircraft used.

Certificate Estimated Flight Hours Estimated Cost (USD) Typical Duration
Private Pilot 40–60 $15,000–$25,000 2–4 months (full-time)
Instrument Rating 35–50 $15,000–$20,000 1–2 months
Commercial Pilot Remaining up to 150–200 total (includes PPL/IR) $30,000–$45,000 4–6 months
Certified Flight Instructor (CFI) Average 10 $5,000–$10,000 1 month
CFI - Instrument (CFII) Average 5-10 $5,000–$8,000 1 month
